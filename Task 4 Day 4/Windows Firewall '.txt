8.Summarize how firewall filters traffic.
A firewall filters network traffic by inspecting incoming and outgoing data packets and comparing them to predefined security rules, allowing or blocking traffic based on criteria like source and destination IP address, port, protocol, and application. It acts as a gatekeeper between trusted and untrusted networks, blocking all unsolicited inbound connections by default while permitting most outbound traffic unless specifically restricted by rules. By enforcing these rules, the firewall helps prevent unauthorized access and protects the computer from malicious activities or threats.
